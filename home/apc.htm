Alameda Point Collaborative (APC)

Branding

Ideas for a lively update to APC’s logo, including the introduction of a branding system to incorporate their social enterprises.

APC was formed in 1999 to help families and individuals break the cycle of homelessness and poverty. APC now provides over 350 formerly homeless residents, including 200 children and youth, with the safety and stability of a place to call home. All residents will continue to have access to life and job skills training and substance abuse and mental health counseling provided by a team of highly skilled professionals.

My goal was to update to make it cleaner and more modern, while also conveying more of the amazing things they do for their residents. Accessibility is key here because to qualify for housing, at least one head of household must be experiencing a permanent disability at entry. In addition, I wanted to create a logo that can be used as part of system to incorporate and standardize the branding for their social enterprises.

I chose a badge logo because they are known to give a sense of belonging to a community, and community is a huge focus at APC. I decided to keep the sun icon from their existing logo because the sun is a universal symbol for new beginnings, which is what APC hopes to provide for its residents.

While I explored a lot of housing-related options, it was not chosen as the final option because APC is more than just housing - it’s an entire system and community that supports their community.

The logo layout with the icon in the center allowed me to create and place icons for the other social enterprises to create their logos. This allows folks to see that the social enterprises are part of APC.

For the color palette, I kept the red for a sense of power and warmth. I darkened it slightly to ensure that it is WCAG AA compliant for normal text. Since Alameda is on an island, I created a sunrise/sunset over the ocean themed color palette.

I used the Acumin Pro families for the logo for its variety of weights. Verdana was a natural choice for the brand typefaces due to its legibility.
