CULTIVATE

Editorial Design

A quarterly magazine student project that cultivates love for houseplants and interior design for newer plant parents.

In recent years, especially after the pandemic, houseplants have surged in popularity. As folks are moving to cities and/or staying in smaller living spaces, they want to transform their apartments, condos, or homes into a sort of “personal oasis”. An article from the Guardian attributes rising interest to urbanization and interior design trends.

Most popular plant and home magazines are marketed to older audiences, are very information heavy, and more outdoor garden focused. I decided to design a chic and informative houseplant and interior designs magazine marketed towards young adults and millennials.

I wanted to feature a lot of images in the magazine, so I felt that a more clean, minimal design would be most suitable. I started with black and white as my main color palette, but decided to introduce a rich, dark green as an occasional pop of color.

I selected Fino Sans as the title typeface. The tall, thin, high contrast font worked well in the masthead, and gave off the luxurious feel that I was trying to achieve. I paired it with Minion Pro for the body text for readability.

For the cover design, I wanted to have an image within a window, so the viewer can “peek” into the issue to see what’s inside and frame the photo as well. I tried various shapes, but ended up going with this arched or tombstone shape. It tied in with the “U” in the masthead nicely, and has a sophisticated feel as well.

The magazine will be printed on a quarterly basis, with Spring, Summer, Fall, and Winter editions each year. The summer issues have a dark green background, as an ode to the abundance of greenery during summertime. The winter issue, when plant activity is a bit slower, has a plant species spotlight with in-depth care on that specific plant.

I used a 6x12 grid to design the inside spread.
