How To Cook Things You Can Actually Eat

Editorial Design

A food allergy and/or dietary restriction friendly cookbook designed for a student project. The goal was to be informative and inclusive, with a touch of humor and color to brighten up a normally complicated topic.

You probably read the title and thought to yourself, “Isn’t this normally the goal of cooking?”.

Growing up in the US, I actually had a really difficult time finding things I could eat, let alone cook. The main culprit were all of my food allergies - milk, eggs, peanuts, tree nuts, soy, and even bananas! With the added complication of having immigrant parents who struggled with English, I was mostly on my own asking restaurants what I could eat or digging through cookbooks in the hopes of finding at least one recipe that worked.

As a tribute to child Jackie and adult Jackie, I wanted to design a modern and trendy cookbook to help folks like me. I challenged myself to make it clearly labeled for those with food allergies and/or dietary restrictions, but still be aesthetically pleasing enough to be on display.

After coming up with a fun title, I searched for a similarly playful font. Hegante Black was perfect for the job, paired with Museo Sans for readability. I selected modern and eye-catching photos for the cover and back by the same photographer for cohesiveness. The flatlay perspective is very trendy and the colors matched the playfulness of the type. Working off the pastels and bright colors in the photos, I chose a bright rainbow color palette that would be sure to pair well with the variety of colors found in all of the food photos.

I designed an 8x10in hardcover book.

The inner pages were designed with a 6x10 grid. I kept the recipe page design relatively minimal, since this is a very photo-heavy book. There are icons in consistent spots to allow the reader to quickly reference the time and cost for each recipe. Each food category is also color-coded, with small colored tabs at the bottom of each recipe for quick sorting.
